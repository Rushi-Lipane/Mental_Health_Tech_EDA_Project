# Mental Health in Tech Survey EDA

## Project Overview
[cite_start]This project explores the human side of the tech industry by diving into the Mental Health in Tech Survey dataset[cite: 1, 4]. [cite_start]Beyond just numbers and code, this analysis is about understanding the real world experiences of people working in tech how their environments, company cultures, and even where they live shape their mental well being[cite: 4, 37].

By following a clear data science path from cleaning up messy, real world data to building detailed visualizations, I have worked to uncover the patterns that matter. [cite_start]The goal is not just to complete a project, but to find meaningful insights that can help companies build a more supportive, open, and empathetic workplace culture[cite: 38, 41].

## Why This Matters

* [cite_start]**The Big Picture:** We look at how mental health trends shift across different countries and regions[cite: 37].
* [cite_start]**Workplace DNA:** We investigate if things like company size or the ability to work remotely actually impact an employee mental health[cite: 17, 18].
* [cite_start]**Support Systems:** We analyze whether having access to employer benefits or a supportive supervisor truly encourages people to seek the help they need[cite: 15, 20, 29].
* [cite_start]**Breaking the Silence:** We explore the comfort levels and stigmas associated with discussing mental health during interviews or with coworkers[cite: 26, 28, 30].

## The Dataset
[cite_start]The data is based on a survey that measures attitudes towards mental health and the frequency of mental health disorders in the tech workplace[cite: 4].

* [cite_start]**Key Columns:** Age, Gender, Country, Family History, Treatment, Work Interference, and Company Benefits[cite: 9, 10, 11, 14, 15, 16, 20].
* [cite_start]**Source:** The original dataset is from Open Sourcing Mental Illness (OSMI)[cite: 40].

## Steps Performed
1.  **Know Your Data:** Initial inspection of rows, columns, and missing values.
2.  **Data Wrangling:** Cleaning non standard gender entries and filtering unrealistic age values.
3.  **Data Visualization:** Creating over 20 logical charts following the UBM (Univariate, Bivariate, and Multivariate) rule.
4.  [cite_start]**Insights:** Identifying the strongest predictors of mental health illness and workplace attitudes[cite: 38].

## How to Run
1.  Clone this repository.
2.  Ensure you have the `survey.csv` file in the same folder.
3.  Open the `Mental_Health_Tech_EDA_Project.ipynb` file in Jupyter Notebook or Google Colab.
4.  Run all cells to see the complete analysis and visualizations.
